Hermana Barton

Sealy Spanish

July 12, 2020 - December 28, 2020

Week 41

Buenos Dias!

This week was pretty good! Thanks for all of the birthday wishes yesterday! My birthday turned out to be an awesome day!!

Last Monday we played pickleball with the district.

Tuesday we taught a recent convert Annie and we had a WhatsApp video call with a man named Naul Rodriguez that we have been messaging on Facebook for a while. The elders found him, but he only speaks Spanish. He lives in Costa Rica, but he still hasn't sent us his address...

Wednesday we had district council! We all went around and shared some miracles or tender mercies that we have seen this year in our lives. I was like "This year sucked!" lol but the biggest miracle that happened to me this year is that I actually came on my mission! I'm so glad that I decided to serve.

Also on Wednesday we went out and shared the Christ Child video with the Martinez family! They loved it. Then they gave us some chicken and lettuce and pico and chips and tortillas and cookies. :)

Thursday we went out to Denny's and taught Ruth the Restoration of the gospel of Jesus Christ. She liked it. We invited her to read the introduction of the Book of Mormon and 1 Nephi 1 and pray about it.

Friday was awful. We were going to teach this new couple that we found on Facebook Summer and Deven. We had shared the Christ Child with them last week and then we talked afterwards and set another time to meet with them! We were planning on teaching the Restoration, but Deven gave us a scripture to read, 2 Timothy 2:22-26. We read it and we thought it was nice, but when we got on the call with him he was like "The snare of the Devil means religion because Jesus hates religion!" And he just kept going off about how we worship the devil because we are part of an organized religion. We weren't arguing with him, but we started trying to explain what we believe but he just wasn't there to learn anything lol. Eventually I said "I'm sorry, but we're going to have to end this call because nothing edifying is happening." and he said "You're trying to control the end of this call, but I'm going to say that you need to go pray to God and ask if God told you to leave your religion, would you?" And then he hung up. I mean it was kinda funny but it was also just terrifying. Hermana Oveson and I just cried and called Sister Guthrie.

The next day, I went through and read all of 1 and 2 Timothy to see if I could figure out where this guy was coming from, and and could see how he could get confused when Paul is talking about people who would teach false doctrines and apostize, but what was kinda funny is the verses that he told us to read were actually about how you shouldn't bible bash with people lol. I really liked reading 1 and 2 Timothy! It talked about how we are all going to pass through tribulation, but that we should hold on to what we already know and remember all the witnesses that we have received. And the entire two books are literally Paul telling Timothy how he can magnify his calling as a leader in Christ's Church (aka, religion) . So I just don't understand how people think that Jesus hates religion...

Anyways

Saturday we went to the library to help with an event they were doing for kids but no one came... so we told the elders and they told a lady that they're teaching Lareeta and then Lareeta came! Later, we went over to Bishop's house and helped them go through pictures that they're going to scan into Family Search.

Sunday was my birthday! It was awesome!! We had ward council at 7am, then church. We had 2 sessions of sacrament meeting. Hermana Oveson and I sang Noche de Luz (Silent Night). Then, we got a ride from Hermana Velasquez to Ven Sigueme at the Ibarra's. We taught the class, then they had food for us. Tamales!!! and they had a cake for me and they sang and I blew out a candle! They gave me a whole bunch of chocolate for my birthday. They're so nice. Elder Satterfield made me some sweet pork so then I made some sweet pork nachos. He also crocheted me a blanket! It's a pretty good blanket. My parents got me literally everything I ever wanted for my birthday so idk what they got me for Christmas.

Spiritual thought:

2 Timothy 1:7-8

7 For God hath not given us the spirit of fear; but of power, and of love, and of a sound mind. 8 Be not thou therefore ashamed of the testimony of our Lord, nor of me his prisoner: but be thou partaker of the afflictions of the gospel according to the power of God;

We do not need to fear or be ashamed of believing in Jesus Christ or in the restored gospel. As we partake of the "afflictions of the gospel", we will learn and grow as we come closer to Christ and progress toward eternal glory.

Les quiero

Love yall!!
