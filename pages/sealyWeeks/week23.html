Hermana Barton

Manvel

April 20, 2020 - July 12, 2020

Week 16

Buenos días familiares y amigos!

Last p day we went biking in Katy in the middle of the day and it was so hot but so fun to actually be with other people lol. I have the worst farmers tan and watch tan line.

On Tuesday I hit my 5 month mark!!! I can't believe it's already been that long. To celebrate we went to this little Mexican shop and I got a mangonada! It was so yummy.

Also on Tuesday we had a devotional with Coach Pope from BYU Basketball and he talked about the importance of being obedient every day and how trials strengthen and change us and how we should always use the word "yet" if we're gonna talk negatively.

On Wednesday we had district council and the ZLs brought us little apple pies from Bucee's. They were so yummy. (But the Bucees in Katy is actually outside our mission and the ZLs didn't realize it lol)

We helped a new family move in to our ward and they have the MOST board games I have ever seen. And half of them were unopened boxes of Monopoly. Hermana West and I just couldn't figure out who needs that many Juegos de la mesa.

We drove over to Columbus on Thursday to get a change of scenery for weekly planning and also to drop off some Books of Mormon. We drove over the Colorado river!!! I thought that was so cool. Only a few thousand miles upriver is Utah!

On Friday we drove down to Eagle Lake and gave out some more Books of Mormon. We were trying to find trailer number 17 for like 20 minutes and it was just about 100 degrees outside and it was miserable. And trailer 18 didnt even exist.

For movie night we watched the first episode of "The Chosen"

On Saturday we had a Zone call on Zoom and played a game called "Dieter or Dumbledore" and you had to read a quote and say whether Elder Uchtdorf or Albus Dumbledore said it. I won.

On Sunday, President Guthrie came all the way out to Sealy for our interviews! We cleaned the apartment like crazy. But I ended up having my interview at the church anyways since we had to head over there for the first sacrament meeting in Sealy since March!

We ended up having 3 sessions of sacrament with less than 50 people at each session and we cleaned everything between them all. Bishop Ramsey and his family are so sweet and brought us sandwiches to eat for helping.

This week is the last week of the transfer!! This transfer has gone by so fast. I'll let you know next week if anyone around me is leaving but we think that Hermana West and I will probably both stay here in Sealy for another transfer.

Spiritual thought:

2 Nephi 26:24

24 He doeth not anything save it be for the benefit of the world; for he loveth the world, even that he layeth down his own life that he may draw all men unto him. Wherefore, he commandeth none that they shall not partake of his salvation.

I think this verse describes very well the love and nature of Jesus Christ towards us. We are all invited to partake of His salvation and to feel of his love and recieve forgiveness of our sins.

I know that Jesus Christ is near me every day. When I first came to Houston, President Guthrie told me that as a missionary I'd be able to feel the Savior beside me more than at any time of my life. I didn't really think much of it. But now I can testify. I know that the Savior of the world works alongside me. I can feel Him near me every second of the day and night. I know He loves me and I love Him.

D&C 26:32

32 Verily, verily, I say unto you, as I said unto my disciples, where two or three are gathered together in my name, as touching one thing, behold, there will I be in the midst of them—even so am I in the midst of you.

Que tengan una buena semana ❤
