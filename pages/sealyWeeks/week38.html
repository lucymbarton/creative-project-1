Sealy Spanish

July 12, 2020 - December 28, 2020

Week 38

Buenos dias a todos

Como estan?

This week was weird with the holiday. Also it rained a lot. And we went over our alloted miles in the truck. We got our friend Erlinda to "church." That's the big stuff!

P day was boring last week since everybody was out of miles and couldn't hang out. Honestly they should give us more miles since the only reason we're using them is to do missionary work... but it's fine. Rumors are going around that Texas is going to shut down with COVID again and if that happens we won't need any of our miles!

Tuesday we went out to Wallis to help our recent convert Melody clean out some of her shelves. She is going to be moving soon but not very far. She only has one leg and loves to talk! It was good service for her because I think she gets lonely in her little house. Her husband is a truck driver and is barely home!

Wednesday we had a lesson with Erlinda! It went really well and I could understand almost everything she said! We were really able to teach to her needs and listen to her concerns! She commited to listen to church! Which is kinda weird because the Zoom of our church is in English so there's not really a way for Spanish speaking people to watch church online... so we just called Erlinda on our phone and gave it to Brother Bolten who was translating for the Spanish speaking people who were at church that day (The Sanchezs and always only the Sanchezs who only speak Spanish)

We had four lessons in a row planned on Wednesday evening, but only one of them happened. But we found a new friend named Mirna and the lady in the Elote truck asked for a pamphlet and gave us free elote! Elote is just corn with mayonaise, cheese, chile sauce, and lemon. It was good for the first few bites but then it was just too much lol.

We taught the Restoration to Jesus, Elvia, Rosa, and the rest of the Medrano family. It was a new experience for me! I have never taught a family that big in person and I had no idea how to teach to all of their needs at the same time. Next time we go over will be better. We had a member there on the phone but it would be better to have a member there in person I think.

Thanksgiving was super fun! In the morning we went to Brother Turner's house and we helped him plant some carrots! He gave us 4 rotisserie chickens and two fat pies from costco. We met up with our district at the church. Hermana Oveson and I brought stuffing, yams, rolls, and pumpkin chocolate chip cookies! Elder Worley made a turkey and Elder Satterfield made mashed potatoes and homemade beef gravy. It was all pretty good. But we had to eat at a table in the parking lot of the church and there were a lot of bugs. And it was weird. The people driving by probably thinks that we are really weird lol. That night the bishop's family invited us over for pie and sat in the backyard and ate some pie and talked about what we're thankful for. Pretty good day.

Saturday we helped Sister Ramsey get her Christmas tree fluffed and other decorations started. We stayed in Brookshire all day to save miles and then we tried to knock on Avainia's door with Giselle (a member who is her friend) but she wasn't home. But we had a good talk with Giselle! She is going on a mission soon!

Como estan ustedes? How was everyone's Thanksgiving?

Spiritual Thought:

Moroni 10:7

7 And ye may know that he is, by the power of the Holy Ghost; wherefore I would exhort you that ye deny not the power of God; for he worketh by power, according to the faith of the children of men, the same today and tomorrow, and forever.

God works by power according to our faith! We are able to develop our faith every time that we try to come closer to God! I invite you all to act in faith this week and wait to see the arm of God revealed in your life!
