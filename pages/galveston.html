Week 43

Buenos dias familia y amigos

This week seemed really long with a lot of new and unfamiliar places and people.

Monday, I left Sealy and came to Galveston! My new companion is named Hermana Koopmans and she is awesome! She is 6 weeks behind me in the mission and she has been here in Galveston for about 3 months. She is from Enterprise, Utah.

Here in Galveston we teach A LOT of lessons! This week we taught 21 lessons! I don't think I've taught 21 lessons in a week since I was back in Manvel. There are a lot of people here. I think the population is somewhere around 50,000. It is a BIG difference compared to all the small towns in my last area. The town of Sealy only had a population of 6,019.

You'd think we'd be able to see the ocean every day, since we live on an island, but we can't... We aren't allowed to drive on Seawall Boulevard and that road is built up a lot higher than the rest of the island and it blocks the view of the ocean. But sometimes in the mornings we can hear the waves crashing when we go out to exercise.

We have a friend named Julianna here who is on date to be baptized! She is 13 and we have been reading the Book of Mormon with her every night. She has grown spiritually so much since the Sisters started teaching her.

On Tuesday when I woke up, I didn't know where I was. I expected to be in the apartment in Sealy, but I was here in Galveston. It was scary lol.

Our district here only has 6 missionaries. Elder Teeples, Elder Peterson, Elder Parker, Elder Ferrin, Hermana Koopmans, and me. Elder Peterson is our district leader and he only has 6 months in the mission! He was at BYU last year and he is a Computer Science major too so we were trying to figure out if we ever saw eachother there but we haven't figured anything out yet.

Hermana Koopmans is a Sister Training Leader this transfer so I got to go on exchanges with Sister Stephens on Friday and Saturday. It was pretty boring. We sent a lot of texts and walked around a park for a long time and didn't talk to too many people. But we got Pho for lunch so it was all good!

The ward here in Galveston is half english and half spanish just like the one in Sealy. At church, I got up and shared my testimony in Spanish and then everyone was really impressed and thought that I was from Mexico. That happens a lot lol.

We found 3 new people to teach this week! Our goal is to find 20 new people to teach this transfer (in the next 6 weeks) and we want to baptize at least 1 person in the next 2 transfers. Julianna is well on her way to her date of February 13th. We just haven't gotten a clear answer from her dad whether we have his permission... hopefully he is fine with it.

Spiritual thought:

Doctrine and Covenants 1:28

28 And inasmuch as they were humble they might be made strong, and blessed from on high, and receive knowledge from time to time.

This scripture is talking about how Latter-Day Prophets and Apostles can receive revelation, but it applies to all of us too. If we humble ourselves and allow the Atonement and Grace of Jesus Christ to work in our lives, we can be made strong, be blessed, and receive personal revelation to help us with decisions we have to make and other things that are going on in our lives!

I know that Christ lives and that we have a living prophet today! I know that the Book of Mormon is true!

Les quiero
