Hermana Barton

Galveston West

December 28, 2020 - March 21, 2021

Week 52

Buenos DÃ­as!

This week was amazing. Last P day we tried to go fishing with the district but it didn't really work out lol no one caught anything. We went to this restaurant called Shrimp N Stuff and it was SO good.

Tuesday we saw the biggest miracle. I still can't even believe that it really happened. We have been meeting with a member's roommate named Andrea for only the past couple weeks. She is Spanish speaking and she moved here from Colombia 2 years ago. We had a lesson about the Book of Mormon and we gave her a copy. She told us on Tuesday that after we gave her the book, she prayed to know if it was something that she should read. She prayed for God to give her a sign or some revelation for her to know that it was His plan for her. And then that day she saw a video from our church on Facebook. "No lo busque!" she said. "I didn't search for it!" It was the answer to her prayer! So that night she started reading the Book of Mormon starting with the introduction, which we had invited her to read, and then she read through the testimony of the 3 witnesses, testimony of the 8 witnesses, AND the testimony of Joseph Smith! She said that she knew that it was true and that so many things had pointed her to our church and to the Book of Mormon and that she wanted to be baptized! Hermana Koopmans and I couldn't even believe what was happening! We couldn't believe that we could be witnesses to such a great miracle. Truly it is His work and we are just like little babies that he allows to be here. I am humbled and trying to be the best instrument that I can be in His hands. We went back on Saturday and she set her date for April 23rd. Andrea is ready and willing to Let God Prevail.

TLDR: Andrea got a miraculous witness of the Book of Mormon and she wants to be baptized in April!

Friday we had exchanges. I stayed here in Galveston and Sister Warren came out to be with me for the day. We taught a lot of member lessons and all of our non-member lessons fell through, but we went walking near a member's house and we gave a Book of Mormon to a kinda scary guy with only one tooth. Sister Warren was trying to explain to him what the Book of Mormon is and he was like "How old are yall? And you think you know more than me a 58 year old man?" And we were like "No sir!" And we tried to get away as fast as we could. Also the Panda Express on the island finally opened on Friday!!! We went in and they gave us flower leis to celebrate their first day being open. It was a good day.

Saturday we taught our English Class for the first time. We had four men come and they were all so nice! Jose, Ronald, William, and Bayron. We went over the English alphabet and how to ask someone for their name and how to spell your name. Hermana Koopmans and I were really nervous about it, but it turned out really well and it was really fun!

Sunday we got Terry and Kimberly at church. Our ward is doing Sunday School and other classes on zoom now after church. Andrea was able to get on the Spanish Sunday school which the Elders taught and she really enjoyed it! And she got to meet some of the Spanish speaking members of the ward! That class was miraculous too.

I will hit one year on the mission this Thursday. I cannot believe that I have already been out for that long. The first few months of my mission seemed like a million years, but now I still feel like I just left home a few months ago. I'm really trying to soak up all of the time and experiences that I am having as a full time missionary.

Spiritual Thought:

Alma 26:12

12 Yea, I know that I am nothing; as to my strength I am weak; therefore I will not boast of myself, but I will boast of my God, for in his strength I can do all things; yea, behold, many mighty miracles we have wrought in this land, for which we will praise his name forever.

I have been taken to pondering lately on the nothingness of man. I reflect back to a year ago, the night before I was to leave on my mission, laying in bed crying and sobbing and wondering how on earth I was going to leave and why on earth I was supposed to do this. It was so overwhelming and seemed absolutely impossible. I thought about just giving up and not going. I wished I could just stop existing so I wouldn't have to be so anxious anymore. But deep down I knew that God would support me. I prayed to know what I should do and He told me that He was there and that He would never leave me no matter what happened. It has been really hard to be a missionary and I would never want to go through it again, but I am eternally grateful for all that I have learned and how much I have grown. He knows me and I am certain that He is preparing me for the rest of my life.

Les quiero

Love yall!
