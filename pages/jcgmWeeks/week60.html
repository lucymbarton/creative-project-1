Hermana Barton

Jones Creek/Grand Mission

April 12, 2021 - July 26, 2021

Week 60

Buenos días! Como están?

This week was full of service opportunities and packing up Hermana Crandall. She will be home today! President called Saturday night and let me know that I will be training this transfer! I will be picking her up on Tuesday and I have no idea who she is, but I am excited! I am spending the night with Sister Stewart until we pick up our new missionaries tomorrow!

Last P-day was pretty fun! We went with the district to Terry Hershey park and walked along the river. Then we went to HEB and all got a pint of ice cream and then we went to a cool park and ate our ice cream and went down a slide. Fun times.

So if you actually read my last email you'll remember that I told Joann that Joseph Smith didn't shoot anyone, turns out that he did and I just forgot about it... But I was a little stressed about how we were going to explain all of this to Joann. I read through the Saints book a little bit and discussed with my companion. And then on Tuesday we had a devotional from President Scott, stake president of the Friendswood stake, and he just had us make a list of all the things that we know are true and then connected everything back to Joseph Smith. So if we know the priesthood power is real, we know that Joseph Smith was a prophet of God because the priesthood was restored through him. If we know that families are eternal, we believe that Joseph Smith was a prophet of God because that truth was revealed through him, etc. It was really awesome and just what I needed to really strengthen my testimony of Joseph Smith so we could explain everything to Joann. However, when we went over to Joanne's this week she told us that she didn't believe God had ever called prophets. She said she thinks Moses and Noah and Abraham were just men. When we invited her to pray about it she said she wasn't willing to. But she's been coming to church for two years so we're still trying to figure out where her concern is deep down.

Thursday we got to have a zone service at our horse place Reining Strength! We all swept and wiped down rails and the outside of horse stalls in preparation for their fundraiser event Derby Days. It was so fun! And the horses were being so cute when we tried to clean their stalls lol. We also were able to go to the Grand Mission relief society activity and we all went around and shared something that we had learned in the past year and it was just so amazing to feel of the other women's testimonies.

We were able to help a new-move-in family paint their house this week and that was pretty fun. But it turned my jeans into painting jeans.

Sunday we had a special multi-stake conference! It was all online, but Elder Soares of the twelve apostles spoke to us! We were able to get 7 of our people to watch it! It was amazing and they talked about love, ministering, faith, and letting God prevail.

I dropped off Hermana Crandall at the mission office last night to go home today! She is ready and she is going to do amazing things. I will see her at BYU in the fall.

Spiritual Thought:

Ether 12:8-9 8 But because of the faith of men he has shown himself unto the world, and glorified the name of the Father, and prepared a way that thereby others might be partakers of the heavenly gift, that they might hope for those things which they have not seen. 9 Wherefore, ye may also have hope, and be partakers of the gift, if ye will but have faith.

I have loved pondering about the power of faith! I know that faith is how we gain access to God's power. I think it makes so much sense because there are a lot of people who don't have access to the priesthood but still see many miracles in their lives because of their faith in God and in Jesus Christ. I think the "Heavenly gift" in these verses means the grace of Jesus Christ and His healing power. I know that I have hope through Him.

Love yall

Les quiero mucho
